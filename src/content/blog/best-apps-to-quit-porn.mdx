## What to Look For in a Porn Recovery App

Before the list: a quick framework for evaluating these apps.

The best porn recovery apps share a few features that actually matter for the neuroscience of addiction recovery:

1. **Urge management tools** — not just a streak counter, but something to actually do when a craving hits in real time
2. **Trigger awareness** — tracking that helps you identify *when* and *why* you relapse, not just *that* you relapsed
3. **Structured recovery plan** — day-by-day guidance through the first 90 days, which is the biologically significant window for dopamine receptor recovery
4. **Accountability or community** — recovery in isolation has significantly worse outcomes than recovery with social support
5. **Neuroscience-grounded content** — educational material that helps you understand what's happening in your brain, which makes the behavioral changes more sustainable

A streak counter alone doesn't do any of this. Here's what's actually available.

## 2. Covenant Eyes — Best for Content Blocking + Accountability

**Platform:** iOS, Android, Windows, Mac
**Price:** Paid subscription
**Primary function:** Screen accountability (not a recovery app)

Covenant Eyes is fundamentally a screen monitoring and accountability tool, not a recovery app. It takes regular screenshots of your device activity and shares them with a designated accountability partner (usually a friend, family member, or counselor).

**What it does:**
- Screenshots and screen monitoring sent to your accountability partner
- Reports of websites visited
- Content filtering (blocks pornographic sites)
- No urge management, recovery plan, or neuroscience content

**What it does well:**
The accountability mechanism is genuine — knowing that someone will see your screen activity creates real behavioral friction against relapse. This is particularly effective if you have a trusted person willing to be your accountability partner.

**What it doesn't do:**
It doesn't help you understand or address the addiction itself. It's a fence, not a recovery program. It also requires your accountability partner to be willing to receive explicit content reports, which is a barrier.

**Best for:** Men who want a hard content block and have someone willing to serve as an accountability partner. Pairs well with POWER (use both simultaneously for blocking + recovery structure).

## 3. Brainbuddy — Recovery App with CBT Focus

**Platform:** iOS, Android
**Price:** Free (limited), paid subscription

Brainbuddy is a recovery app with cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) elements. It includes streak tracking, trigger identification, and CBT-based exercises for managing urges.

**What it does:**
- Streak counter and daily check-ins
- CBT exercises and journaling prompts
- Trigger tracking
- Educational content

**What it does well:**
The CBT-based exercises are genuinely useful. Brainbuddy draws from evidence-based behavioral therapy more explicitly than most apps in this category.

**What it doesn't do:**
Personalized recovery plans are less developed than POWER. The educational content is solid but not as deeply grounded in the specific neuroscience of pornography addiction.

**Best for:** Men who are already familiar with CBT and want a recovery app with that specific framework.

## 5. NoFap App (Streak Counter) — Basic Tracking Only

**Platform:** iOS, Android
**Price:** Free

The "NoFap" branded apps available in app stores are primarily streak counters. They track days since last relapse, sometimes with a basic badge/reward system.

**What they do:**
- Streak tracking
- Basic statistics
- Sometimes community links

**What they don't do:**
No urge management, no recovery plan, no educational content, no trigger tracking. A streak counter alone has minimal evidence for improving outcomes — it measures recovery without supporting it.

**Best for:** Men who already have a full recovery program in place and want a standalone counter. Not sufficient as a standalone recovery tool.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the best free app to quit porn?**
POWER is free to download with all core features accessible at no cost, including the streak tracker, urge management tools, and base recovery plan. It is the most fully featured free option specifically built for porn addiction recovery.

**Is there a NoFap app?**
Yes — the NoFap community has associated apps, but most are basic streak counters. POWER is built on the same principles (abstinence, neurological recovery, 90-day reboot) with significantly more recovery infrastructure.

**What app do most people use to quit porn?**
POWER has 250,000+ users and is the most widely downloaded app built specifically for pornography addiction recovery. Covenant Eyes is widely used for screen accountability specifically.

**Do porn recovery apps actually work?**
Apps work best when they provide structure, urge management tools, and accountability — not when they're just streak counters. Research on behavioral addiction consistently shows that structured recovery programs with accountability have significantly better outcomes than willpower alone. An app that provides structure and tools is a meaningful intervention; a streak counter alone is not.

**What about blockers like Cold Turkey or Freedom?**
Content blockers (Cold Turkey, Freedom, BlockSite, iOS Screen Time) are separate from recovery apps — they restrict access at the device or browser level. They're valuable as an environmental tool but don't address the neurological and behavioral patterns underneath the addiction. Combining a blocker with a recovery app like POWER is the most complete approach.
